Literature Review

In today's society, anxiety and depression are becoming increasingly troublesome mental health disorders, and they appear to be especially severe in industrialized and developing nations. Depression is the most severe form of sadness, and it is well acknowledged as a significant role in lowering people's effectiveness and as the leading cause of suicide. Suicidal thoughts and even suicide attempts can occur when serious mental diseases go untreated. Cyberstalking and cyberbullying are kinds of harassment that occur over the internet and directly impair a person's mental health. The consequences may be serious and harmful, since individuals who engage in social cruelty are more likely to be involved in gossip and even mental injury. There is a correlation between cyberbullying and suicide, according to research. Victims who are continually exposed to hurtful words or events may become sad and despondent, and in the worst-case scenario, they commit suicide.

Most people like this reach out to online social media platforms to express their concerns just to get some help or to share their feelings. When people openly discuss their feelings on social media without worrying about the social stigma, it can be assumed that it represents their true feelings. Usually, there are many posts with sensitive content in such forums, indicating that the user is at risk of suicide and self-harm. In that theory, many researchers are now focusing on developing systems that can automatically identify people at risk for suicide by conducting an accurate study of these data. The urgency of these systems can be seen in focus on contemporary society.

Hence, the major purpose of the text analyzing part of our research study is to look at individuals' social media posts to see if they have any depressive thoughts. Machine learning-based classification approaches and Natural Language Processing (NLP) techniques used to social media posts for training the data and then evaluate efficiency for early detection of suicide ideation. The Multilayer Perceptron (MLP) classifier best reveals the combined features' power and efficacy (LIWC + LDA + bigram). Furthermore, this provides the best results for the identification of depression, with 91% of accuracy along with 0.93 F1 scores.

To assess and compare it to other classification models, a LSTM, GRU model has been used. The best applicability classification results can be obtained using a combination of word embedding methods and neural network architecture according to our research. Furthermore, the findings demonstrate that deep learning architectures may be used to develop an effective model for assessing suicide risk in a variety of text categorization tasks.Images with text (texted images) contains valuable information same as content-based images (non-texted images). There are very few research regards the detecting suicide related images but contain very important information although they are not directly similar. Images on webs, social media, and in databases are increasing in every single minute. Therefore, it is very valuable to implement effective techniques to filter and manage these by the content of multimedia materials

Research Gap

There is text detection research in natural scene images robotically which named “USTB_TexStar”. Nowadays there are many systems to identify text in day today life such as traffic warning and advertisement board text. When compared to other image features, text embedded in photos can directly expose the picture content in a given point of view without requiring extensive processing. They used the MSER-based scenario text detection method in this case. Initially, MSER's pruning algorithm was utilized to recognize most characters even when the image was of poor quality, and then a self-training distance metric learning algorithm was presented to simultaneously find distance weights and clustering threshold. Then they built a strong scene text detection system by combining the above new strategies and creating a more powerful text classifier. There has been a few research on the use of image-based social media as intervention methods for suicide-related imagery, but the results were mostly exploratory.

One of other research about non suicidal self-injuries but not exactly about suicide related images using Instagram (NSSI), They used a dataset with 30,000 labeled instances of NSSI to train the ACoL model and compared the output results with two pre-trained models which are VGG16 and ResNet50. The ACoL reached classification accuracy of 94% and outperformed other modern Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) models. The ACoL outperformed other recent CNN models with a classification accuracy of 94%. They created a content crawler to collect data that scans Instagram for images, texts, and videos using hashtags on a regular basis and saves them to cloud storage. Using an Instagram crawler, they gathered over 1.45 million NSSI-related posts and downloaded over 1.7 million photos. After they classified those images into six 3 categories by extracting the hashtags associated with NSSI-related posts. To successfully recognize NSSI photos, a deep learning-based binary image classifier was utilized for image classification.

When considering the image data generation because there are no sufficient image datasets for this suicide related image scenario, Augmentation through simple image alteration is the naivest way of data generation. To create new data, this method utilizes original images using image translation, noise addition, and rotation. These approaches are also included in our model to boost robustness and perform better.

Methodology

Analyze Text Data of Suicide Emotions

The purpose of the suicide text analysis research component is to enhance the performance of text classification and language modeling system for detecting suicide ideation in several social medias with the use of a machine learning classifier. This section contains a detailed discussion of methods that employ a number of NLP and text classification techniques. Convolutional and single recurrent neural networks are used as vectors to encode a whole sequence, on the other hand, tend to miss key information sequences. Consequently, several experiments were done to establish a hybrid framework for coherent combinations of CNNs and RNNs to use merits of both. Specifically, the text analyzing research component contributes in three ways: N-gram analysis: Use n-gram analysis to find out suicidal ideation and reduced social engagements are widely discussed in suicide-related forums, as well as to see if the transition from self-focused attention to social ideation is linked to manifestations of hopelessness, heightened self-focused attention, frustration, , loneliness, or anxiety. Analysis of classical features: Using LSTM and GRU analyze the performance of the bag of words, TF-IDF, and statistical features over the word embedding. Comparative evaluation: We studied the function of the LSTM and GRU deep neural networks like the recommended model for identifying suicide ideation tasks, to develop the state-of-the-art method. The proposed Framework includes feature extraction, data pre-processing and the uses of natural language processing (NLP) approaches to encode words for use by classic machine learning systems. Pre-processing is the practice of filtering text input to enhance the validity of a recommended technique by removing extraneous information in order to handle all the raw posts beforehand learning of word embedding. Before moving on to the training phase of our research, we engage the Natural language toolkit (NLTK) to pre-process the dataset. Using LSTM and GRU neural network architecture to create a unified LSTM, GRU model for classifying selected text data in order to identify the suicidal thoughts on social media. Following that it creates a new GRU and LSTM model extract the attributes of the input text phrases and increase the accuracy of classification.

Suicide-Related Image Detection

The key factor that had to be examined in the domain of suicide-related image identification was user behavior recognition utilizing their uploaded images. This had to be a novel way because there had never been a procedure that used a Generative Adversarial Network (GAN) to identify a user who was depressed or attempting suicide. Various research has concluded that a considerable amount of data is required for the success of CNN-based learning algorithms. However, in practice, such a large dataset is not always available. GAN is an unsupervised learning technique in machine learning, and it was helpful because there was not a sufficient dataset. Developing a GAN requires both discriminator and generator CNN models. The discriminator model is used to determine if an image is real or not. The generator model is used to generate new images that may have been generated from the original dataset. The model of generator learns to generate new convincing suicide-related images and discriminator model that tries to differentiate real suicide-related photos in the training dataset with new images generated by the generator model.



As the first step, we defined a discriminator model by inputting images of sample from dataset and obtain a classification forecast as output to determine if the sample data is real or not. The model of discriminator contains two convolutional layers, each with filters of 64, a small kernel size of three, and a stride of two. The model uses an activation function of sigmoid to determine if the input sample is real or fake, and it has been trained to reduce the binary cross entropy loss function, which makes it ideal for binary classification. Best practices were used in this discriminator, such as the use of Dropout, the Adam version of stochastic gradient descent including learning rate of 0.0002, momentum of 0.5, and the use of 'LeakyReLU' by instead 'ReLU.' Furthermore, developed a generator which, responsible for generating new plausible, fake suicide related images. Main focus of this generator model is to generate new plausible fake samples against the discriminator model’s performance on fake images distinguish. Meanwhile, training the generator model through logical GAN model, the purpose is to convince the discriminator that the samples generated by the generator are real. The discriminator model updated in batches, contains a set of real samples and another set of produced samples. On training, an epoch is defined as a single run across the entire training dataset. After defining the model, loaded the dataset, and trained the discriminator. After developed the GAN, it used in the social media post uploader component in the social media application to detect suicide related images

Key logger

A key logger was implemented to capture the keystrokes from the social media web application. This key logger can capture all keystrokes entered by the user. Sensitive information provided by users will not be stored in any of the databases because the key logger was created solely for the purpose of identifying suicidal intent. Before being sent to the suicide prediction model, all the keystrokes are pre-processed. Before being fed to the suicide prediction model, pre-processed key logs are fed to a sentence generation learning model. Google’s T5 model has been used for generating sentences. This learning model predicts the possible sentences from the key logs which are fed to it. Since the key logger captures all key logs, there may be times when the user deletes messages and then retypes them, causing the key log records to be out of order. In the recorded key logs, there may be a sequence of characters followed by a delete key or backspace, spaces, and quotation marks. It would be a huge problem if such records were introduced into the learning model without any pre-processing since they are useless. Data pre-processing and Google's T5 model will be utilized to construct meaningful phrases from the characters captured by keyboard logger in order to address that issue. The produced phrases are then retrieved from the T5 model and fed into the NLP model that predicts suicidal ideation. This NLP model will be trained on a huge dataset that includes both suicidal and non-suicidal material.
